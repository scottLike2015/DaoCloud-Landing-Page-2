Still Have Questions About Daocloud?

Read through our Frequently Asked Questions

How will people find me on DaoCloud?

We are promoting DaoCloud through a robust affiliate program. Affiliates are generously rewarded for promoting DaoCloud to their extended networks.

We enable search engine optimization for all of the information on your profile, so you will be easily discoverable by anyone searching for a wellness professional online. Anyone (not just members) can search the map page by type of professional, location, specialties, and/or conditions treated.

More importantly, you will be promoted through your clients' profiles. The more clients you connect with, the more you amplify word-of-mouth!

Who can leave testimonials on my profile?

Members and professionals need to request to connect with you, and you need to accept the connection request before they can leave a testimonial. This way, you can protect yourself from competitors leaving false reviews.

Will my list of clients be public?

No - any and all information regarding the relationship between you and your client is completely private. Your clients have the choice to promote you on their profiles, but your list of clients is not made public on your profile.

Is DaoCloud HIPAA compliant?

Yes. Our legal partner, Womble Carlyle, is assuring that DaoCloud is entirely HIPAA compliant. Remember, members will not be sharing sensitive medical records. Members will share resources and inspiration that improve their health.

How secure is the information on DaoCloud's platform?

DaoCloud uses the same industry standard security protocols as most financial institutions.

What’s the meaning behind the name "DaoCloud"?

DaoCloud represents a philosophy of balance integrated with technology. “Dao” comes from the Dao (or Tao) Te Ching. “Cloud” represents cloud technology.

Is DaoCloud 'against' conventional medicine?

No - we believe everyone has something valuable to offer. We recognize the benefits that conventional medicine has in treating acute conditions. However, chronic health conditions affect one in two Americans, and we recognize the potential that self-care has to reverse this trend. Some say holistic therapies and self-care are 'complementary and alternative' to conventional medicine. This, we disagree on. We believe self-care is the most effective form of healthcare. Conventional medicine, in our eyes, is complementary to self-care.
